Questions

Frequently asked

What exactly is a "season"? ↓
A season is one complete crop cycle from planting to harvest. For most vegetables like tomatoes or cabbage, that's 10–16 weeks. For longer crops like ginger, it's 6–8 months. You pay once per cycle. If you want to plant a second crop (different or same), you buy another seasonal plan — or upgrade to Full Farmer for unlimited.
Can a beginner really use this? ↓
Absolutely — that's the core promise of the Seasonal plan. When you select "Beginner" during setup, AgriLedger makes all the key decisions for you: row layout, spacing, spray programme, fertiliser schedule. You follow along step by step. If you're unsure about anything, the AI agronomist explains it in plain language, no technical background needed.
What crops are supported? ↓
At launch: tomato, strawberry, cabbage, onion, pepper, ginger, maize, and groundnut. We're adding more based on what our early farmers grow. If your crop isn't listed, the AI agronomist can still advise you — and we'll prioritise adding it in the next update.
What's the difference between single, double, and triple bed rows? ↓
These describe how many plant rows fit across one raised bed. A single-row bed has one row of plants down the centre — best for crops that need maximum airflow like large tomatoes. A double-row bed has two rows — the most common setup, balancing yield per metre with access. Triple rows fit three rows — best for dense crops like onions and groundnuts where you walk between beds rather than into them. AgriLedger shows you a visual diagram and recommends the best option for your crop automatically.
Does my data carry over if I upgrade to Full Farmer? ↓
Yes. Every harvest log, spray record, and crop cycle from your Seasonal plan is available the moment you upgrade. Nothing is lost — it all becomes part of your farm history that the AI uses to give you better advice over time.
Is AgriLedger available outside Zambia? ↓
We're launching in Zambia first. Zimbabwe, Kenya, South Africa, and Ghana are next. The platform is built to support any market — local currencies, local crops, local market prices. If your country isn't listed, join the waitlist and tell us where you are. That helps us prioritise.
What happens to my data if I don't renew? ↓
Your data is yours. If you don't renew, you can export everything — your harvest records, financial logs, and spray history — as a PDF or spreadsheet before your plan expires. We hold it for 90 days after expiry so you have time to decide.
